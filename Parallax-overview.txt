PARALLAX — what it is, why it's different, and the ask
Prepared for: Tuesday conversation with a fintech CEO (friend)
========================================================


--------------------------------------------------------
1) WHAT MAKES PARALLAX SPECIAL
--------------------------------------------------------

THE ENGINE (the part nobody else builds this honestly)

- Real history, not made-up returns.
  Monte Carlo runs on ACTUAL inflation-adjusted market returns
  from 1928–2025 (98 years). Source: Aswath Damodaran's dataset.
  No invented "average return" assumptions.

- Block bootstrap, not random coin-flips.
  It samples real CONTIGUOUS stretches of history, so a bad
  decade stays a bad decade. Crashes, recoveries, and the ORDER
  they happen in are preserved — that's sequence-of-returns risk,
  the thing that actually breaks retirements.

- 8 real asset classes.
  US large, US small, international developed, emerging, US bonds,
  cash, REITs, gold — each with its own real return history.

- Three account buckets.
  Taxable, Traditional, and Roth modeled separately, with
  withdrawals pulled in a sensible ORDER across them.
  (A full tax engine is NOT built yet — that's planned, not a
  claim to make Tuesday.)

- Full lifecycle in one path.
  Accumulation (still working + saving) -> retirement -> pension
  start -> Social Security timing -> long-term-care shock -> death.
  Every Monte Carlo path is internally CONSISTENT — the same life
  lived through one coherent market history.

- Historical "what if you retired into ___" mode.
  Run the exact same plan as if it started in 1929, 1966, 1973,
  1987, 2000, 2008, etc. — real markets, real order.

- It's verified. The engine has a full test suite (42 tests,
  all passing). It's pure math, isolated from the interface, so
  the numbers can't silently break when we change the visuals.


THE PRODUCT (what the advisor and client actually see)

- SCENARIOS tab — compare CHOICES side by side.
  Columns = scenarios (a Baseline + alternatives). Each is topped
  by a live Monte Carlo SUCCESS % with the delta vs baseline the
  instant you run. Move the levers and watch them interact:
    * Retirement age
    * Social Security start age
    * Annual spending
    * One-time spending event (amount + age)
    * Portfolio allocation
    * Annual savings (pre-retirement)
    * Per-scenario GOAL amounts (edit any goal per column)
  An expected-wealth line chart shows each scenario's path in
  today's dollars. One-click "Reset" snaps everything back.

- SEQUENCING tab — same plan, different MARKETS.
  Hold the plan fixed and run it through real historical market
  orders (retire into 1973 vs 2000 vs 1995...). Shows sequence
  risk directly — why TWO identical plans can end up worlds apart.

- Goals as a priority board, real net-worth / cash-flow inputs,
  and a year-by-year cash-flow view that's just a window onto the
  engine's own output (not separate, re-derived math).


THE PHILOSOPHY (the real differentiator)

- It models INTERACTIONS, not isolated stats. The value is the
  client SEEING one lever move another (e.g. a more aggressive
  portfolio raises the expected line but LOWERS the success % —
  that trade-off is the whole point).

- It is NEUTRAL. Every other retirement tool is built around fear.
  Parallax has no thesis. The same gauge reads green when the news
  is good and red when it isn't. It's equally willing to say
  "you're fine, spend more" as "this won't last, cut back."
  That neutrality is exactly why an advisor can trust it in front
  of a client.

- The program shows the TRUTH; the advisor tells the story.
  No canned "you might feel anxious" sentences. If the visual is
  right, the narration is redundant — so we cut it.


--------------------------------------------------------
2) WHAT PARALLAX IS FOR (its purpose)
--------------------------------------------------------

Parallax is a retirement-planning simulator for financial
advisors — built by a working CFP (personal-CFO model, 200+
households, $300M+ AUM), not by a software shop guessing at the
problem.

Its purpose: show clients the TRUTH of their retirement as close
to reality as the math allows — how time, markets, taxes, goals,
behavior, and decisions interact — so that better understanding
creates better judgment.

It is NOT a "highest ending balance wins" calculator. It solves
for what actually matters: spending security, a cushion against
bad market timing, surviving long life and health shocks, and
meeting the goals that matter to the household.

In one line:
  Every other tool sells fear and hides the upside.
  Parallax runs real history honestly and reports what's there —
  good news and bad — which is what makes it trustworthy in the
  room with a client.
