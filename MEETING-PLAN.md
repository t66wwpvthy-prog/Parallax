# Parallax — Meeting Plan

**Date:** 2026-06-04  
**Format:** Zoom  
**Purpose:** Gut-check from someone whose opinion matters. No ask. No pitch. Just an honest read.

---

## Phase 1 — Why we're here

"I built something and I want to know if you think it's real. That's it. No ask, no pitch — just your honest read."

---

## Phase 2 — What it is and why nothing else does it

Every retirement tool is built around a bias. Conservative outputs, fear-based defaults, isolated numbers that don't talk to each other. I built Parallax because I wanted a tool I could actually trust in front of a client — one that runs the real math and reports what's there, whether the news is good or bad. The engine runs 98 years of real market data. It models three account types correctly. It shows you how decisions interact, not just what the number is.

**Keep this short. You know the tool inside out — there's a pull to over-explain. Resist it. Say the one sentence about bias, show him the thing, and let him form his own opinion. The tool makes the case better than the explanation does.**

---

## Phase 3 — Where it goes

Retirement planning and tax planning are the same conversation. Nobody has connected them in one honest model. That's where this is going. Not there yet — but it's clear.

---

## Tool walkthrough

**3 moves. That's it.**

1. **Set the household.** Age, retirement age, spending, portfolio. A real scenario he can relate to. Let him watch the success circle populate.

2. **Move one lever.** Change spending or retirement age. Let him see the number react and the line shift. Don't explain it — let him feel it.

3. **Ask him.** "What would you expect a tool like this to do next?" Then stop talking.

---

## The reminder

You're not training him on the software. You're giving him just enough that his brain starts working on it. The moment he leans in and asks "wait, what happens if you change the allocation" — that's him selling himself. That's worth ten full demos.

Less is more here specifically because he's smart and he's in the industry. He'll fill in the gaps faster than you can explain them. Your job is to light the fuse, not detonate the whole thing.

If he wants more, he'll ask. And that ask tells you exactly where his head is.
